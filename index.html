SCHOOLS OF THE FUTURE

Through our road trip we wish to raise awareness and funds for EWB NL’s initiative: “Schools of the Future”. This project’s aim is to teach children and communities how technology can improve their lives. Educational packages, with a focus on engineering and entrepreneurship, will be designed and distributed in developing areas. Our experience from the classes will provide valuable input for these packages and the project. The hope is that many will be inspired to start small technology driven companies.

 

Support “Schools of the Future” by making a donation below. Please note that every cost incurred during our trip (Back to Japan) is covered by the team members. All funds raised will be donated to Schools of the Future, upon completion of our project. Thank you for supporting this worthy cause!

2015 L TEAM PRODUCTIONS

The project started with an 18 year old Honda Civic named “Lastochka”. “Lastochka” means “Swallow” in Russian.  Swallows migrate during the winters but always return home. After many years of good service in Norway it was time for Lastochka to return home. Our team set out on the road trip of a lifetime.

 

In cooperation with, the international NGOs: “Engineers Without Borders” and “World Education Foundation”, a series of lectures has been set up. These interactive lectures aim to promote technical education through demonstrations and sharing of personal experiences

 

During our previous travels we have been overwhelmed by the kindness, generosity and hospitality that we encountered in various countries. This inspired us to start L Team Productions. Throughout our “Travel for Change” project we plan to make a humble contribution to the countries we visit.

OUR PARTNERS

OUR TEAM

The team consists of three professional engineers with an inter­national background and strong communicational skills. All are currently working for the largest Nordic engineering company and travel with the project during their time off. They have been top students (MSc/PhD) from highly recognised universities and have lecturing experience in technical subjects.

CLASSES

The lectures aim to show how meaningful and interesting engineering is and are designed for students aged 10-14 (grade 5-9). Interaction with the students and demonstrations make for an exciting program capturing the attention of the students. Several practical exercises have been prepared in which the students get hands-on experience in mini engineering projects. For example: the design, building and testing of a wind turbine and bridge model.
